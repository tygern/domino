Element Explorer

Compute properties of elements in the type \(D\) Coxeter group. An element of \(D_n\) is a signed permutation \([w(1), w(2), \ldots, w(n)]\) with an even number of negative entries. The group is generated by reflections \(s_1, \ldots, s_n\), where \(s_1\) swaps and negates the first two positions and \(s_i\) (\(i \ge 2\)) swaps positions \(i{-}1\) and \(i\). The generators satisfy the Dynkin diagram

\[s_1 \,\backslash\!\!\stackrel{}{\phantom{s}} s_3 - s_4 - \cdots - s_n \,/\!\!\stackrel{}{\phantom{s}} s_2\]

where \(s_1\) and \(s_2\) both connect to \(s_3\) at the branch.
